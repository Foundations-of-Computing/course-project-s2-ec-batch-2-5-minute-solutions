Step 1: Check Startup Programs

Too many programs launching on startup can slow things down. Disable unnecessary ones.

  • Task Manager (Ctrl + Shift + Esc) > Startup tab
Step 2: Disk Cleanup

Remove temporary files and system clutter to free up space.

  • Disk Cleanup tool
Step 3: Defragment Hard Drive (HDD)

If you have an HDD, defragment it to improve performance. (Skip for SSDs)

  • Defragment and Optimize Drives tool
Step 4: Update Drivers

Outdated drivers can cause performance issues. Update your graphics, chipset, and other drivers.

  • Device Manager or manufacturer's website
Step 5: Check for Malware

Malware can significantly slow down your computer. Run a full system scan with antivirus/anti-malware software.

  • Use a reputable antivirus program.

Unclog a Drain Naturally

You'll Need:
  • ½ cup baking soda
  • ½ cup vinegar
  • Boiling water
Step 1: Pour baking soda down the drain.
Step 2: Slowly add vinegar - it will fizz!
Step 3: Wait 15 minutes while it works.
Step 4: Flush with boiling water.
Pro Tip: For tough clogs, repeat the process twice and use a plunger gently after pouring the boiling water.

Replace Bulb With Ease

Quick Start:
  • Check the Power Supply
  • Circuit Breaker/Fuse:
  • Damaged Socket
Step 1: While not everybody follows this step, its the safest way to avoid electrical shock or injury risk
Step 2: If the bulb was recently on, it may be hot. Allow it to cool down completely before touching it to avoid burns. If your light bulb is incandescent or halogen, it may be too hot to touch safely with your bare hands.
Step 3: In most cases, simply twist the bulb counter-clockwise until its released. If it doesnt twist easily, use thick gloves or a tool to grip it.
Step 4: Double-check the sockets wattage rating before installing the new lightbulb. If its equal to or below the maximum amount, carefully twist the bulb into place. It should turn easily dont try to force it if it doesnt fit properly. You may need to try a different shape or size.
Step 5:
  • Properly dispose of the bulb, as some bulbs cant go into the trash.
  • Incandescent and halogen bulbs can technically go in the trash.
  • However, as theyre made of glass that could injure a sanitation worker, its recommended that they be wrapped in newspaper or cardboard.

New knob, smooth open

Step 1:
  • Remove the old door knob, latch, and strike plate.
  • Install a latch plate on top of the latch
  • Slide the latch inside the edge of the door
  • Use a wooden block and a hammer to tap the latch into place.
  • Secure the latch into the door with 2 screws.
Step 2:
  • Secure the first half of the doorknob in through the latch
  • Align the other half of the door knob with the first one
  • Push the two sides of the door knob together
  • Attach both door ends with screws
Step 3:
  • Use wood putty to secure any loose screws
  • Screw in the new strike plate
  • Test the door knob to make sure it works
  • Refinish or repaint the area around the door knob, if needed
